Relativity Theory Explained

Relativity theory is a theory proposed by Albert Einstein in the early 20th century, which fundamentally changed our understanding of space, time, and the nature of gravity. There are two main components to relativity theory: special relativity and general relativity.

General Relativity Theory Explained

General relativity is a theory of gravity proposed by Albert Einstein in 1915. It describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime caused by the presence of matter and energy. The basic idea is that massive objects, like planets and stars, curve the fabric of spacetime around them, causing other objects to follow curved paths through space.

Special Relativity Theory Explained

Special relativity is a theory proposed by Albert Einstein in 1905, which fundamentally changed our understanding of space and time. The basic idea behind special relativity is that the laws of physics are the same for all observers moving at constant velocities, and that the speed of light is constant for all observers, regardless of their relative motion.
